The 5 Ws & How

WHAT

Motivation is the collection of factors affecting what people choose to do, their effort, and persistence. Includes Intrinsic (internal) and Extrinsic (external) factors.

WHY

Crucial for performance. Surveys show ~70% of employees are not fully reaching their potential. Engaged employees drive organizational success.

WHERE

Applied in organizational policies, specific job designs (rotation/enrichment), and individual team dynamics.

WHEN

Continuously. Use Hygiene factors to stop dissatisfaction and Motivators to start satisfaction. Use Equity theory when fairness is questioned.

HOW

By designing meaningful jobs (Enrichment), setting Specific & Challenging goals, and using Positive/Negative Reinforcement.

Lecture Topic Tree

Motivation

1. Fundamentals

  • Intrinsic vs. Extrinsic
  • Disengagement Statistics (70% gap)

2. Needs-Based Theories

Maslow's Hierarchy:

Physiological → Safety → Social → Esteem → Self-Actualization

Herzberg's Two-Factor:

Hygiene (prevents dissatisfaction) vs. Motivators (creates satisfaction)

ERG (Alderfer):

Existence, Relatedness, Growth (Simultaneous pursuit allowed)

McClelland's Acquired Needs:

Achievement, Affiliation, Power

3. Process-Based Theories

Equity Theory:

Comparing inputs/outputs to others (Fairness)

Expectancy Theory:

Effort → Performance → Reward

4. Strategies

  • Job Design: Rotation, Enlargement (Variety), Enrichment (Depth)
  • Goal Setting: Specific, Challenging, Achievable, Feedback
  • Reinforcement: Positive vs. Negative

Maslow vs. ERG Theory

Difference:

Maslow requires completing lower needs before moving up. ERG allows pursuing multiple needs at once (e.g., salary + growth).

Hygiene Factors vs. Motivators

Herzberg's Theory

Hygiene (Salary, Policies) prevents dissatisfaction but doesn't motivate. Motivators (Recognition, Achievement) actually drive satisfaction.

Job Enlargement vs. Enrichment

Width vs. Depth

Enlargement adds more tasks (variety). Enrichment adds more responsibility and control (depth).

Expectancy Theory Formula

The Chain of Belief

1. Effort leads to Performance.
2. Performance leads to Reward.

Negative Reinforcement

Definition

Removing an unpleasant stimulus to encourage good behavior (e.g., stopping nagging when a task is done).

Goal Setting Criteria

What makes a goal work?

It must be Specific, Challenging, Achievable, and offer Feedback.
