Module 9: Culture

5 Ws Dimensions Flashcards

Culture & Diversity

Understand the invisible forces that shape organizations.

The 5 Ws & H

WHAT

Culture is shared values/beliefs. Diversity is the mix of backgrounds/styles.

WHY

Competitive advantage, branding, recruiting, resilience, and creativity.

WHERE

Seen in "Artifacts" (dress, layout) and felt in "Assumptions" (unconscious beliefs).

WHO

Founders (Disney, Watson) set the vision. Leaders role-model the behavior.

HOW

Maintained via Hiring/Onboarding. Changed via Urgency/New Leadership.

Dimensions of Culture

Detail-Oriented Precision (Ritz-Carlton)
Innovative Risk-taking (Google)
Aggressive Competition (Microsoft)
Outcome-Oriented Results (Best Buy)
People-Oriented Fairness (Southwest)
Team-Oriented Collaboration (Zappos)
Stable Rules/Order (Gov/Banks)
Customer-Oriented Service (Amazon)

Exam Flashcards

Artifacts (Schein)

Definition:

The visible signs of culture. Dress code, office layout, rituals, and awards.

How to Initiate Change

3 Key Steps:

1. Create a sense of urgency.
2. Role modeling (Leaders act it out).
3. Change leaders if necessary.

Onboarding

Maintenance Method

The process of orienting new employees to the company policies, values, and culture.

Underlying Assumptions

Deepest Level of Culture

Unconscious, taken-for-granted beliefs that guide behavior. Hardest to see or change.

Diversity Benefits

Advantages

Higher creativity, better resilience, improved company image, and better morale.

Temperance

IBM Value

A virtue emphasizing moderation and self-control. Part of Thomas Watson's original IBM culture.

Study hard! Remember the examples: Google (Fun) vs. IBM (Formal).
