Module 14: Communication

The 5 Ws Topic Tree Flashcards

Effective Communication

Mastering the flows, channels, and barriers of business interaction.

The 5 Ws & H

WHAT

The process of exchanging info: Sender → Encode → Channel → Decode → Receiver.

WHY

Provides clarity, builds relationships, creates commitment, and defines expectations.

WHERE

Flows Downward (Instruction), Upward (Feedback), Horizontal (Peer), and Diagonal (Cross-level).

WHEN

Use Oral for emotional/complex issues. Use Written for official records.

HOW

It fails via Barriers: Filtering, Selective Perception, Overload, and Semantics.

Lecture Topic Tree

Communication

1. The Process

  • Sender (Encoding) → Message → Receiver (Decoding)
  • Noise: Distractions that distort the message
  • Formal vs. Informal: Official structure vs. Social "Grapevine"

2. Direction of Flows

Vertical Downward (Manager to Staff)
Upward (Staff to Manager)
Lateral / Cross Horizontal (Peer to Peer)
Diagonal (Different Dept & Level)

3. Barriers (The Enemy)

  • Filtering: Hiding bad news (Sender)
  • Selective Perception: Hearing what you want (Receiver)
  • Semantics: Confusing Jargon
  • Info Overload: Too many emails!

4. Channels

  • Oral: Rich, immediate feedback (Face-to-face)
  • Written: Lean, permanent record (Email, Reports)
  • Active Listening: Eye contact, focus, empathy

Exam Flashcards

Click a card to reveal the answer.

Filtering

Sender Barrier

When a sender intentionally manipulates information (like omitting negative numbers) so the receiver sees it more favorably.

Diagonal Communication

Flow Direction

Communication between employees at different levels AND in different departments (e.g., Marketing Associate talking to Finance Director).

Active Listening

The Solution

Listening fully to the speaker without interrupting. Includes eye contact, reading body language, and asking clarifying questions.

Semantics

Language Barrier

Differences in the meaning of words. Often caused by technical "jargon" that the receiver doesn't understand.

Selective Perception

Receiver Barrier

When the receiver hears only what they want to hear based on their own needs, motivations, or background.

Rich vs. Lean Channels

Channel Selection

Rich: Face-to-Face (High feedback, emotional).
Lean: Written Reports (Low feedback, factual/static).
