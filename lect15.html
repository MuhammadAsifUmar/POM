The 5 Ws & H

WHAT

A team is 2+ people with a shared goal and mutual accountability. Key concept: Social Cohesion vs. Social Loafing.

WHY

Pros: Creativity, shared workload, better problem solving.
Cons: Conflict, slower decisions, unequal effort.

WHERE

Functional: Same dept.
Cross-Functional: Mixed depts.
Virtual: Remote locations.

WHEN

Tuckman's Model: Forming → Storming → Norming → Performing → Adjourning.

HOW

Build effective teams via clear Communication, establishing Norms, and enforcing Accountability.

Lecture Topic Tree

Groups & Teams

1. Core Dynamics

  • Social Cohesion: Emotional bonds/cooperation.
  • Social Loafing: Doing less work in a group.
  • Collective Efficacy: Belief in ability to succeed.

2. Types of Teams

Structure Functional (Same Dept)
Cross-Functional (Mixed)
Operation Self-Managed (No boss)
Virtual (Remote)

3. Tuckman's Stages

  1. Forming: Polite, unclear roles.
  2. Storming: Conflict, frustration.
  3. Norming: Trust, clear roles.
  4. Performing: High efficiency.
  5. Adjourning: Completion.

Social Loafing

Negative Behavior

When a group member exerts less effort because they are in a group (relying on others) than they would alone.

Storming Stage

Stage 2

Marked by conflict, disagreement, and frustration as members clash over roles and working styles.

Cross-Functional Team

Team Type

Members from DIFFERENT departments working toward a common goal (e.g., Marketing + Engineering + Sales).

Norming Stage

Stage 3

When conflicts resolve, trust is built, and clear roles/norms are established. The team starts to cooperate.

Collective Efficacy

Positive Behavior

The shared belief among team members that they are capable of organizing and successfully reaching their goals.

Self-Managed Team

Team Type

A team that operates with little or no supervision, managing their own tasks and decisions.

Good luck! Remember the order: Form, Storm, Norm, Perform.
